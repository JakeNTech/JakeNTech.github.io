CyberStart Elite 2019

A vist to SkyGarden, a week in the capital and a SANS FOR500 course!

Late last year (2018) I was invited to continue Cyber Discovery, after having completed year 1 and making it through to elite. When Game began, I saw that a new base had been added, a forensic one; I began work on this straight away. Trying to balance coursework was tricky (Due to the amount of work that needed to be done, and the duck was sometimes just too appealing to spend an afternoon playing with!) but I managed to squeeze it all in along with Cyber Discovery. After having completed to the forensic base, I moved on to continue with my progress with game, I ended Game with 98% Completion (I simply ran out of time) although this was higher then my completion last year I felt as if it wasn't enough, as many other people had completed all of it. After Game, Essentials rolled out and we were given the chance to re-do the end of module tests, to which I started to do, but it soon became apparent that I would not be able to juggle the work needed at the time. I sat down and did all I could, under the illusion that I probably had not done enough to make it to the next phase.

Of all the content that was given on Game and Essentials the one that I found the most enjoyable was forensics, I can't explain why other then it was just interesting!

I am sure you can imagine my surprise when (During some Computer Science revision) I got an email congratulating me on making it through to the elite phase! I kid you not I had to re-read the email a few times to make sure that I had indeed seen it correctly. However, it was true. I really had made it through to the elite phase! (YAY!)

I chose to take the FOR500 course, as it looked to be the most appealing to me; it was also being hosted in London, which is easy to get to from home. But because of the start time it would mean traveling in at rush hour, something both me and my dad wanted to avoid at all costs, so we booked into a hotel in Farringdon the day before, it also made traveling on the train cheaper. I was kinda nervous about the week ahead, spending a week with people who I had only really spoken too online a few times, I wasn't very active on the community discord server. However those thoughts ran away as we got to sky garden. Check in the next day was at 9 ish, and so in the rain we walked the two(ish) miles to university.

Once I had been checked in and had a chat with a tonne of people, who where all super friendly, we where shown into the room where the course was going to be fed to us; We had the awesome Kevin Ripa as our instructor, who made the course even more exciting. Huge amounts of valuable information was shared with us about digital forensics and computing in general. The windows registry was one of the most enjoyable things taught, IMO.

Up until a few weeks ago the registry was something that could potentially break my PC if I started tinkering with it too much (And a way to activate windows XP on test VM’s); but now it’s a source of all sorts of information that can giveaway what you were up to on a system, such as shellbags and user assist. Some of the other super interesting things that we were taught where o-alerts and browser forensics. I also picked up that Windows 10 phones home a lot and that in-private browsing isn’t all to private.

On the last day we had the opportunity to take on a real world example of a case to solve, which was super intense but was great to put what we had been taught to good use, unfortunately our team did not win, but my god it was good fun! If anything, it has made me more determine to keep on going!

Another truly amazing part of the course was that we where all given a Forensic Computer Write-Blocker; this prevents any connected drive from having data written to (Essential in an investigation). I have a pile of old IDE and SATA II drives that could hold a lot of information, and soon I will be able to find out some stuff about the younger me, and possibly carve out some deleted files that went missing all them years ago!

For me this week was invaluable for what I want to do going forward. Not only was this week great for this course, it was amazing to meet like minded people. My confidence was boosted no end, I feel truly proud to have attended such an event!

I cannot wait to move on and start studying this full time at university!

I am hoping to start teaching myself some more to do with forensics, such has how to fully understand a email header and do some research into how the Windows time-line can be used in a forensic way (If it can be used)

Before I close this post off I would like to take the time to thank CyberStart/CyberDiscovery for hosting/helping me to get to such a fantastic event! Lastly, to Kevin who made the week something which will not be forgotten in a hurry, and helping me to find a passion for this subject! And to this day Kevin is still helping us with Forensic questions and supporting us with preforming our own investigations on our own drives.

Thanks again for all the people involved with the organization of this event! I am hornerd to have attened such a fantastic event! I truly cannot wait to start forensicating! It's just ashame that I am now too old to do such an epic program again!

EDIT: Now I have completed the GIAC exam and I am happy to report that I passed! Yay!
